## Summary

The waitlist page is strong for a private alpha: the first viewport communicates the pain, the form is above the fold, the app preview makes the product feel real, and the trust routes reduce privacy/support anxiety. The largest pre-audit gap was not UX mechanics; it was SEO and search-intent specificity. The page used creator-native language, but it did not put enough exact-match language in high-value places for queries like "Twitch subscriber tracker", "Prime sub tracker", "Twitch CRM", and "monthly subscriber review".

## Findings

### Medium: Search Intent Was Slightly Too Implicit

Psychology model: Jobs to Be Done + availability heuristic.

Creators searching for this problem are more likely to search for concrete workflow language: subscriber tracker, Twitch CRM, Prime drops, spreadsheet, monthly review. The page now makes the job explicit while keeping the emotional opening.

Status: fixed.

### Medium: Bottom Of Page Needed A Second Prompt

Psychology model: BJ Fogg Behavior Model + activation energy.

On mobile, a reader who consumes the FAQ had to scroll back to act. The added bottom CTA catches users after objections are answered and reduces effort.

Status: fixed.

### Low: Social Proof Is Appropriately Missing For Now

Psychology model: social proof + authority bias.

There are no testimonials or creator logos yet. That is fine for a private alpha; fake proof would reduce trust. Add 2-3 creator quotes only after alpha users give permission.

Status: keep as-is until real alpha feedback exists.

### Low: Scarcity Is Ethical And Clear

Psychology model: scarcity + regret aversion.

"Small creator cohort. Feedback-first." works because it is true and does not pressure users with fake urgency. This should remain grounded in the private alpha scope.

Status: keep.

## UX Notes

- Primary task cost: two fields and one submit button.
- Main decision point: "Is this actually for my creator workflow?" The Built for / Not built for sections answer that well.
- Trust anxiety: reduced by Privacy and Support links plus FAQ language about no automatic messages and no exact Prime expiry dates.
- Mobile layout: no visible horizontal scroll in screenshot pass; the form remains early enough in the page.
- Page tone: concise, creator-focused, and still inside the positioning guardrails.
